Primary Typeface

SAY HELLO TO MARK

Solid, strong, friendly, and confident.

COPY TONE/ VOICE

Write the way you would speak to someone. Think less car salesman or legal department, and more friends catching up over drinks.

HEADLINES

Mark OT Black
ALL CAPS/UPPERCASE

TL;DR

Too Long; Didn’t Read. In the age of Twitter updates it’s best to be concise and to the point. People don’t have the time, and literally cannot be bothered, to read paragraphs of text. It becomes overwhelming and counterproductive.

If you can say something in five words instead of fifteen, then do so.

Distilling our copy down not only helps us to pinpoint exactly what we’re trying to say/sell, but also helps audiences pick out the message that much faster.

A friendlier voice also helps the message stick in people’s minds.

LEGIBILITY

The key is legibility!

If the piece would scale down and still conform to the rules above, then, generally speaking, it will work. Note: we must ensure the minimum font sizes are respected when scaling artwork down. It may look right in relation to surrounding type, but double check to ensure minimum sizes are respected.

The transparency of legal lines on ads can be reduced to ~50%, so that they are still legible, but light enough that they don’t distract from the main messaging on any given piece.

On coupons, vouchers, etc. do not reduce the opacity of the legal lines as they are the main focus on those pieces.

LEGAL COPY

Mark OT Book

Copy: Sentence case

5-6pt*
Regular sized materials (smaller than a stanchion/trumpet sign)

*Mark appears larger than some other typefaces and so it may be used at sizes as small as 3.5pt (eg. on the backs of Gift Cards). Use reasonable judgement – ensure it will be legible on the piece you’re creating. If you find that you have to go below 5pt to make the legal copy fit, switch to Mark OT Condensed.

Printers may flag a file when scanning for font sizes as they will see anything under 5-6pt as questionable/hard to print. Using Mark any smaller than 5pt should be avoided if possible.

12-18pt
Medium-to-large pieces (from stanchions to movie posters to backlit transit posters)

24pt +
For construction and hoarding walls, or exterior banners.

Body Copy

Mark OT Bold

Body Copy: Sentence case | CTA/URL: Title Case

Body copy on ads should be limited to two lines of copy (avoiding bullet points), including the Call to Action (CTA)/URL. If the message requires two full lines of copy, separate out the CTA/URL onto its own (third) line, away from the main body copy.

Ensure that the URL ends the sentence rather than burying it in the middle of a sentence.

Example 1:

Improve your spy game this November.
Watch the trailer at Cineplex.com/Spectre

Example 2:

Improve your spy game with SPECTRE this November in UltraAVX.

Watch the trailer at Cineplex.com/Spectre

URL TREATMENT IN ADS

The ‘C’ in Cineplex should always be uppercase. All of the text that comes after the forward slash should be changed to an accent colour to help call out the URL (use a colour to compliment the program and the background). Also, each word in the URL copy should have its first letter capitalized to aid in legibility/readability and consistency across English, French, and Bilingual pieces.

Example 3

Cineplex.com/MovieGiftPack Cineplex.com/PochetteCadeauCinema

Example 4:

Brand.Cineplex.com

The callout word will be highlighted, with its first letter capitalized.

URL TREATMENT IN PARAGRAPHS

When a URL appears in the middle of a paragraph, or in legal type, the capitalization rule still applies, however the text can all remain one colour (the same colour as the rest of the copy in the paragraph). The highlighted colour is reserved for more prominent URLs in ads

If the URL needs to be given more prominence within a paragraph, instead of changing the colour, the entire URL should be made bold, so that it will stand out from the rest of the paragraph (helpful in black and white ads).

Example 5:

Subject to the terms and conditions on the back of the Card. Issued by Cineplex Entertainment. Treat this as cash. Visit Cineplex.com for theatres, movies, showtimes, and more.

LENGTH/FORMAT

Headlines should (generally) be restricted to 1-2 short lines (8-10 words or less), and should convey a value or ammenity, and relate to a movie or program.

    • Example 1

    See your movie in UltraAVX, where you’ll experience big screens, big sound, reserved seating, and more!

    Example 1 is trying to say too much and reads like a bulleted list. If literal callouts to features are desired, limit their use to body copy, not in headlines.

    Example 2

    See your movie
    in UltraAVX.

    Example 2 fails to tell you anything about the movie, what UltraAVX is, or what it can offer (If space is limited and there is no movie tied to the ad, this format may be used, however literal callouts to the program should be left for the body copy, not headlines).

    Example 3

    Big sound.
    Very big sound.

    Example 3 gives a nod to the 007 Bond franchise while also telling you something about the UltraAVX experience.

    HEADLINE STYLES GRADIENTS/REFLECTIONS

    Depending on the art style of the piece, a gradient may be applied to the headline text to create more depth. The same principle applies here as is does on background gradients – the brighter colour should be more prominent from the center toward the edges, and the darker colour should only come into play closer to the outside edges of the copy.

    A subtle reflection can be placed on the last line of a headline. Reflections should not be applied to all lines, just the bottom line.

    The bottom line reflection should fade out by half of the character’s height and the opacity of the layer can be reduced to 30% (or less) to ensure a subtle effect is achieved.

    A reflection not manditory on all pieces. for instance, if the piece leverages flat colours, or if the piece is too small, or will only be viewed from very far away where the effect wouldn’t be clearly visible, the reflection man be omitted. Use best judgement.

    Mark Condensed

    The normal version of Mark should be used wherever and whenever possible before the Condensed version is applied. Mark Condensed is to be used when there are extreme limitations in space. Primary: Legal type Secondary: Skyscraper web ads, Certificates

    Archer

    Where is appropriate to use archer. What’s the value in it?

    Where mark is solid, strong, stable, Archer provides a lighter, fancier, and more fluid alternative, which helps to offset the weight and impact of Mark.

    Core Magic

    Where is appropriate to use archer. What’s the value in it?

    When should you use Core Magic? Headlines mainly. Possible on ‘click here’ buttons... As a Slab serif, Core Magic is solid, with more customizable character combinations.

    It should be used when we want to make a statement, or creatve an alternate style for a campaign in a way that has more potential for variation than Mark.
