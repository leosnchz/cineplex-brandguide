Grid System

Page Divisions

Primary: 65 / 35%
Secondary: 80 / 20%

The page divisions are used to separate image from text, ensuring a clean/consistent division of information within a piece.

When possible, the 65/35 split should be used over the 80/20 split. This spacing is the default for Magazine ads, Movie/Transit Posters, etc. However, the amount of copy, the orientation of the piece, and the image itself helps dictate when and where to use the split, especially on web assets and less conventional ad specs.

PAGE DIVISIONS vs COLUMN GRID

The division is (traditionally) accompanied by a gold line, and a clear change in colour from the background; that is, if the image/art is dark, use a lighter colour for the footer, and vice versa. The two sections can still be dark (or both light) as long as the division is clear.

Web assets often depend on these splits to help organize information consistently across projects, when we move into print, and larger ad spaces, we introduce the twelve column grid to help structure each piece.

Primary: 65% / 35%

Secondary: 80% / 20%

Logo Positioning

12 COLUMN GRID

After setting up page trim, bleed, and safety, and before starting in on the design, apply a twelve column grid to the page.

By default on print ads, all copy and logos must remain within the innermost ten columns, while the single outermost columns remain empty to ensure each piece is allowed to breathe within the safety margins / visual opening (think of matting on a picture frame).

Print - Larger than 8.5" x 11"

Establish page trim, bleed and safety. Set up the twelve column grid, and use the innermost ten columns, keeping the outermost columns free from text. Text and/or logos near the edges of a piece should have as must space between themselves and the page trim to give the impression of a consistent border around the piece.

PRINT - SMALLER THAN 8.5” x 11”

Establish page trim, bleed and safety. Set up the twelve column grid.

With smaller pieces we often cannot afford the loss of space those two extra columns provide (most evident in copy-heavy pieces), so make use of all columns (if needed).

WEB

With smaller pieces (ROS, Facebook, Twitter), it’s more important to establish the 65/35 or 80/20 split (detailed on next page) and work within the trim/safety margins, than to strictly follow the column grid.

SCREENS

Digital screens should follow the same rules as print and web assets, maintaining the outer column safety as a guideline. With digital screens it is often easier to follow the web guidelines where the percentage page split is prioritized over the 12 column grid.

Logo Sizing

When Cineplex is alone, use the full colour option with Gold Rings whenever possible. The logo can take up three columns to help fill the space.

When centered for operational signage, or on exterior banners, etc. the Cineplex logo should appear in full colour with Gold Rings whenever possible, and can take up to four or five columns.

Use sizing that will complement the piece you are working on, balancing available space and volume of copy.

When paired with another logo the Cineplex logo should revert to a colourless version (to allow the hero logo to shine). The Cineplex logo should take up two columns, and be separated from the hero logo by a thin vertical line.
