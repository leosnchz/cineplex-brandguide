introduction

What

The Brand Guidelines document provides information on grid and colour systems, typographic and copy considerations, brand positioning, program logos, and best practices for all of Cineplex’s properties If there are specific concerns or areas that are not coverered in this guide, please contact the deisgn team: CreativeProjects@Cineplex.com

Who

Everyone Anyone who will be working with a Cineplex Brand Designers (in-house and freelancers), Marketing, Executives, promotional partners, Design and Construction, etc Being familiar with Cineplex Brand Guidelines will facilitate clearer communication between all parties and help ensure more streamlined messaging and consistent content

Why

Cineplex is an ever-growing company with a wide range of properties In order to maintain brand consistency across all programs and initiatives, including a streamlined hierarchy of messaging and visual elements, it is essential to adhere to standardized Brand Guidelines

While each program has its own look and feel, adhering to consistent Brand Guidelines ensures that a piece is recognizable as a Cineplex property no matter what it is communicating, and/or where it is located Consistent branding leads to an increase in overall brand awareness.

How/When

Clients: Anyone who will be requesting a project should familiarize themselves with Brand Guidelines to help structure (and focus) their main message (See: Communication Framework)

Designers: Anyone who will be working on a project request should be well versed in Cin- eplex Brand Guidelines People that request projects will not always be thinking about branding per se, so it is the responsibility of the design team to apply appropriate stan- dards to any project If unsure about branding, designers should contact their direct manager for clarification and direction

Communication Framework

When requesting or creating a new piece of art, it’s important to think about where it fits in relation to other programs

Who is your target? What is your desired outcome? What should the driving force of your copy be?
