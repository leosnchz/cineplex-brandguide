Brand Logo Guidelines

Spot the Differences

OLD BRAND

  • Darker/muddier colours
  • Rings(larger than Cineplex 'C')
  • Rings rounded/narrower on lower end
  • Slightly wider letters

NEW BRAND

  • Brighter vibrant colours
  • Rings(same hight as Cineplex 'C')
  • Sharp edges on bottom of rings mirror top
  • Slightly narrower letters

Spacing

At all times, any use of Cineplex (or Cineplex affiliated brand) should be used while leaving the full “C” space around the logo

  • Logos should remain on a clean solid or lightly textured background.
  • Logos should always be used with Gold rings whenever possible, but if not, knockout version is acceptable.
  • Dark Silver logos should only be used in logo trains, backs of coupons and at times where it’s positioned/paired with a product logo.
  • Cineplex branded versions of logos should always be used in external messaging seen outside of our theatre or not on our website (when not already paired with the Cineplex master brand logo).
  • Non-Cineplex branded Product or Service logos should always be used within our buildings or our website.
  • The Cineplex Rings should never be scaled or sized differently than in the original logo file.
  • The Cineplex Rings should not be moved to the sides or below a logo.
  • Logos should never be rotated, placed on an angle, skewed, or otherwise distorted.
  • Do not cover logos with any other co- lour than their pre-versioned formats; within the Cineplex colour pallete or otherwise.
  • Logos should not be covered with a texture, material, pattern, or gradient.
  • Logos should remain a solid colour.
  • Logos should never have Stroke, Inner Shadow/Glow, Pattern Overlay, Gradient Overlay.
  • Cineplex should always appear with Rings. Exceptions must be approved by brand team. (NOTE: Refer to ‘Wordmarks’ section)
  • Dark Silver may be used on the backs of coupons, in logo trains, on 3rd party gift card backers, and on the Cineplex logo when paired with an- other product where Cineplex is not the focus of the ad.
  • Corporate Blue may be used on Corporate Events, Corporate Products such as 3rd party gift card backers, or the Cineplex Logo Train Lockup.

Cineplex Rings

BACKGROUND

The Cineplex Rings icon represents a symmetrical graphic depiction of the ancient Greek amphitheatre or “Odeon” which is Greek for the word theatre.

These theatres were the place where communities came together to be entertained. The circular rings stand for entertainment and unity. The rings are now a unifying icon, ready to be applied to multiple properties acting as a connective element for Cineplex.

WHEN/WHERE TO USE

The Cineplex Rings on their own, separated from a wordmark, should be used sparingly, and only as a subtle accent element.

  • Logos should remain on a clean solid or lightly textured background.
  • Logos should always be used with Gold rings whenever possible, but if not, knockout version is acceptable.
  • Dark Silver logos should only be used in logo trains, backs of coupons and at times where it’s positioned/paired with a product logo.

  • Non-Cineplex branded Product or Service logos should always be used within our buildings or our website.
  • The Cineplex Rings should never be scaled or sized differently than in the original logo file.
  • The Cineplex Rings should not be moved to the sides or below a logo.
  • Logos should never be rotated, placed on an angle, skewed, or otherwise distorted.
  • Do not cover logos with any other co- lour than their pre-versioned formats; within the Cineplex colour pallete or otherwise.
  • Logos should not be covered with a texture, material, pattern, or gradient.
  • Logos should remain a solid colour.
  • Logos should never have Stroke, Inner Shadow/Glow, Pattern Overlay, Gradient Overlay.
  • Cineplex should always appear with Rings. Exceptions must be approved by brand team. (NOTE: Refer to ‘Wordmarks’ section)
  • Dark Silver may be used on the backs of coupons, in logo trains, on 3rd party gift card backers, and on the Cineplex logo when paired with an- other product where Cineplex is not the focus of the ad.
